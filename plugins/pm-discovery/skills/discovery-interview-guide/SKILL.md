# Skill: discovery-interview-guide

## Trigger
Use this skill when the user wants to prepare for user research interviews during a discovery — e.g. "prépare le guide d'entretien", "génère un interview guide", "j'ai des interviews clients à faire", "prepare my user interview guide".

## Context
At Mayday, the Observe phase (step 2 of FOCUSED) involves conducting qualitative research through client and internal interviews. The goal is to deeply understand the problem space and identify the main pain point (First Use Case / FUC). A good interview guide:
- Is structured but leaves room for natural conversation
- Focuses on understanding current behaviors and workarounds, NOT on validating a solution
- Always ends with a prioritization exercise ("what's the most important problem for you?")
- Has a clear time allocation per section

## What to do

### Step 1 — Gather context
Ask the user for the following if not already provided:
- What is the discovery topic / problem area?
- Who is being interviewed? (client / internal: Success, Sales, etc.)
- What are the key learning goals from the Kick Off? (paste them or summarize)
- Approximate interview duration (default: 45–60 min)

### Step 2 — Generate the Interview Guide

Produce a complete interview guide. Adapt the language to match the user's (French if they write in French).

---

**[Interview type] Guide — [Discovery topic]**
*Duration: [X] min | Persona: [persona]*

---

**📋 Before the interview (interviewer prep)**
- Reminder: do NOT present solutions or lead the witness
- Goal: understand current reality, not validate our ideas
- Take notes on: exact words used, emotional cues, workarounds described

---

**🙏 Introduction (3–5 min)**
Script:
> "Merci de prendre le temps de nous parler aujourd'hui. On est en train d'explorer [topic] et on voudrait vraiment comprendre comment vous travaillez aujourd'hui — pas vous vendre quoi que ce soit. Je vais vous poser des questions ouvertes, il n'y a pas de bonne ou mauvaise réponse."

---

**Section 1 — Context & Role (5 min)**
Questions to understand who they are and their day-to-day context:
- [3–4 questions tailored to the persona and topic]

---

**Section 2 — Current Behavior & Pain Points (15–20 min)**
The heart of the interview. Questions to understand how they currently handle the problem:
- [5–7 questions following the "current behavior → pain → workaround → business impact" arc]
- Include at least one question about workarounds: "What do you do today when X happens?"
- Include at least one question linking the pain to business: "What's the impact when this doesn't work well?"

---

**Section 3 — Priorities & Hierarchy (10 min)**
Force the interviewee to prioritize:
- "Among everything we've discussed, what's the most critical problem for you today?"
- "If you could fix only one thing, what would it be?"
- "Why that one over the others?"

---

**Section 4 — Tools & Ecosystem (5 min)**
- "What tools do you use today to handle this?"
- "What do you like / dislike about them?"
- "Have you looked at alternatives?"

---

**🎯 Closing (3–5 min)**
- "Is there anything important we haven't discussed that you think we should know?"
- "Would you be open to testing a prototype when we have one?"
- Thank them

---

**📝 Post-interview: things to capture immediately**
- Top 3 pain points mentioned
- Exact quotes worth saving
- Workarounds described
- Surprise/unexpected insights

---

## Output format
- Produce the full guide, ready to use
- Include time allocations per section
- Write in the user's language
- Bold the key questions
- Add brief interviewer notes in italics where useful
- Adapt sections to the persona (client interview vs. internal interview have different tones)
